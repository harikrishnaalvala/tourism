Charminar

The Charminar is a monument located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. Constructed in 1591, the landmark is a symbol of Hyderabad and officially incorporated in the emblem of Telangana. The Charminar's long history includes the existence of a mosque on its top floor for more than 425 years.

Golconda Fort

Golconda was the principal capital of the Qutub Shahi kings. The inner fort contains ruins of palaces, mosques and a hill top pavilion, which rises about 130 meters high and gives a bird's eye view of other buildings. Golconda fort is undoubtedly one of most magnificent fortress complexes in India.

Salar Jung Museum

Salar Jung Museum is an art museum located in Hyderabad, India, known for its vast collection of art and artifacts from around the world. It houses over 40,000 objects and is considered one of the most important museums in India.

Ramoji Film City

Ramoji Film City is an integrated film studio facility located in Hyderabad, India. Spread over 1,666 acres, it is the largest film studio complex in the world and as such has been certified by the Guinness World Records. It was established by Telugu media tycoon Ramoji Rao in 1996. The Guardian described Ramoji Film City as "city within a city".

Hussain Sagar Lake

Hussain Sagar Lake is a historic lake located in the heart of Hyderabad, India. It is one of the largest man-made lakes in the country, named after a revered Sufi saint, and is surrounded by several important landmarks, including the Necklace Road, Lumbini Park, and Buddha statue.

Balkampet Yellamma Temple

One of the old and among the most revered temples of Hyderabad is the Yellamma Temple located at Balkampet, popularly called the Balkampet Yellamma temple. The temple is thronged by heavy crowd on Sundays and Tuesdays and is quite famous for the annual Bonalu Jathara festivity that takes place in Hyderabad. This temple is dedicated to Goddess Yellamma that literally means 'mother of the universe'.

Charminar

Charminar is a historical monument located in the heart of the old city of Hyderabad, India. The name "Charminar" is derived from two words: "char" meaning four, and "minar" meaning tower. The monument was built in 1591 by the fifth ruler of the Qutub Shahi dynasty, Muhammad Quli Qutub Shah, to commemorate the end of a deadly plague that had gripped the city.
The Charminar is an impressive example of Islamic architecture, blending Persian and Indian styles. The structure consists of four minarets, each 56 meters high, connected by arches and a central dome. The minarets are decorated with intricate carvings and have balconies that offer stunning views of the surrounding area.
The Charminar is also known for its unique acoustics. It is said that a clap made at the base of one minaret can be heard at the top of the other minaret.
The monument is situated in the middle of a bustling market, which is famous for its jewelry, bangles, and traditional clothing. The market is known as Laad Bazaar, and it is one of the oldest and busiest markets in Hyderabad.
Charminar has a rich history and has witnessed many important events in the city's past. It was the site of the first public meeting of the All India Muslim League in 1924, which was chaired by the founder of Pakistan, Muhammad Ali Jinnah. It was also the site of many protests during the Telangana movement, which aimed to create a separate state of Telangana.

Golconda Fort

Golconda Fort is a historical monument located in the city of Hyderabad, India. It is situated on a hilltop, about 11 kilometers from the city center. The fort was originally built by the Kakatiya dynasty in the 13th century, but it was later fortified and expanded by the Qutub Shahi dynasty in the 16th century.
The fort is a popular tourist destination and is known for its impressive architecture and rich history. The structure is built on a granite hill that rises to a height of about 120 meters. It consists of four distinct forts that are surrounded by a 10-kilometer-long outer wall. The inner forts were built to protect the royal family and their treasures, while the outer wall was built to protect the entire city.
The fort has several gateways, each of which is an architectural marvel. The most famous of these gateways is the Fateh Darwaza, which means "Victory Gate." It is one of the main entrances to the fort and is decorated with intricate carvings and stucco work. Legend has it that when the gate was built, it took 8 years to complete, and the workers used a total of 2,000 oxen to transport the construction materials.
Another interesting feature of the fort is its unique acoustic system. The fort has a series of hidden passages and chambers that were used for communication and surveillance purposes. The acoustics of these passages were so advanced that a clap made at the entrance of the fort could be heard at the highest point of the fort, over a kilometer away.
The fort also has several important structures within its premises, such as the Taramati Mosque and the Golconda Palace. The Taramati Mosque is an impressive structure that was built in the 17th century. It is known for its unique architecture, which combines Hindu and Islamic styles. The Golconda Palace was the residence of the Qutub Shahi rulers and is now in ruins. However, it still retains its grandeur and is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the region.
The fort played an important role in the history of India, especially during the Qutub Shahi dynasty. It was a major center for diamond trading and was famous for its diamond mines. The famous Koh-i-Noor diamond, which is now a part of the British Crown Jewels, was mined from this region.
Today, the fort is a popular tourist attraction and is visited by thousands of people every year. It has been declared a UNESCO World Heritage site and is a proud symbol of the rich cultural heritage of India.

Salar Jung Museum

The Salar Jung Museum is a museum located in the city of Hyderabad, India. It is one of the largest museums in the country and is known for its impressive collection of art, sculptures, manuscripts, and other historical artifacts. The museum was established in 1951 by Mir Yusuf Ali Khan, also known as Salar Jung III, who was a nobleman and art collector.
The museum houses over 43,000 artifacts, including Indian, European, and Middle Eastern art. The collection includes paintings, sculptures, textiles, ceramics, carpets, and furniture, among other things. Some of the most famous exhibits in the museum include the Veiled Rebecca, a marble statue that is considered to be a masterpiece of European art, and the Jade Room, which contains a vast collection of jade artifacts from China.
The museum is spread over three floors and is divided into several sections. The ground floor houses the Indian art section, which includes artifacts from the Mughal, Rajput, and Deccan periods. The European art section is located on the first floor and includes paintings and sculptures from the 18th and 19th centuries. The second floor houses the Middle Eastern and Far Eastern art sections.
One of the most interesting things about the museum is that it reflects the personal taste of Salar Jung III. He was a passionate collector of art and artifacts and acquired most of the items in the museum himself. His eclectic taste is reflected in the collection, which includes everything from ancient manuscripts to modern art.
Apart from the exhibits, the museum also has a library that houses over 9,000 books and manuscripts. The library is open to scholars and researchers and is an important resource for the study of art and history.
The museum also has a conservation department that is responsible for the preservation and restoration of the artifacts in the collection. The department uses advanced techniques and technology to ensure that the artifacts remain in good condition and are protected for future generations.
The Salar Jung Museum is an important cultural institution in India and is visited by thousands of people every year. It is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the country and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in art and history.

It is one of the largest man-made lakes in the country and is known for its beautiful surroundings and historical significance. The lake was built in the 16th century by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah, the founder of the city of Hyderabad.
The lake is named after Hussain Shah Wali, a revered Sufi saint who is believed to have lived in the area during the reign of Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah. The lake is located at the center of the city and is surrounded by several important landmarks, including the Necklace Road, the Lumbini Park, and the Buddha statue.
The lake is spread over an area of 5.7 square kilometers and has a maximum depth of 32 feet. It is fed by several streams and channels and is connected to the Musi River, which flows through Hyderabad. The lake is surrounded by several parks and gardens, making it a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.
One of the most iconic landmarks of the lake is the Buddha statue, which stands at the center of the lake on a small island. The statue was installed in 1992 and is one of the largest monolithic Buddha statues in the world. The statue is 16 meters tall and weighs over 350 tons. It is a popular destination for tourists and is beautifully lit up at night.
The lake is also home to several recreational activities, including boating and water sports. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake and enjoy the beautiful views of the surrounding areas. The lake is also a popular destination for fishing, and several species of fish can be found in the lake.
The lake is an important source of drinking water for the city of Hyderabad, and several measures have been taken to ensure its conservation and protection. The government has implemented several measures to reduce pollution and maintain the quality of the water. The lake is also surrounded by several parks and gardens, which serve as a green lung for the city and help to maintain the ecological balance.
Overall, Hussain Sagar Lake is a beautiful and historic destination that is an important part of Hyderabad's cultural heritage. Balkampet Yellamma Temple

This temple is dedicated to Goddess Yellamma that literally means 'mother of the universe'. There is also an alternative name called Jagadamba who is considered the same as Goddess Renuka. According to a legend, Renuka, the daughter of Raja Renuka had married Jamadagni, the son of Satyawati and had five sons, one of them being Parashurama. One day, Renuka couldn't perform one of her regular household chores and this enraged her husband who then asked her to leave the house.Dejected Renuka was reportedly suggested by saints to perform pujas near a Shivalinga and feed Brahmins for her penance. Jamadagni was reportedly furious at her and demanded his sons as well to punish their mother Renuka. Others refused and they were burnt to ashes by the father's gaze while Parashurama searched for his mother who was hiding in a village, beheaded her and even the poor woman who helped her. Jamadagni who was pleased by his son's obedience, gave him a boon and in turn Parashurama asked his father to restore the lives of his brothers, his mother and the village woman. However, during the process, the head of Renuka was restored on the other woman's head and vice versa. While Jamadagni accepted the woman who had Renuka's body, the other form came to be popular known as Yellamma, which means mother to all and is being devoutly worshipped from then by the people ofTelangana people. Balkampet Yellamma Goddess is also considered as an incarnation of Goddess Kali. Devotees offer animal sacrifice here. The most important event here is the celestial marriage ceremony of the temple goddess, Yellamma Kalyanotsavam. Bonalu Jathara Festival at Balkampet Yellamma Temple falls in the calendar only after Golconda Bonalu Jathara followed by Ujjaini Mahankali Bonalu Jathara each year. The temple was first constructed in 15th Century AD and later renovated in the early 20th century. The idol of the goddess here is seen 10 feet lower than the ground level and the devouts believe that the water in the well here can cure all ailments. The holy water is treated as Theertham. The temple also has Akhand Jyothi, which was lit during the renovation. It is situated at a distance of 10 km from Secunderabad Railway Station and is just 3 km away from Ameerpet junction.
